Research Highlights

Hybrid In-situ Nanofibrillation & Crosslinking of Polymer Composites

Pioneered innovative techniques across multiple polymer systems (PP/EPDM, HDPE/TPU, PMMA/PEBA), achieving 200-400% increases in toughness and elongation while maintaining strength, stiffness, and transparency through controlled nanofibril formation (50-150nm) and selective crosslinking mechanisms.

Breakthrough Impact: Enhanced mechanical performance without trade-offs

Advanced Aerogel Fiber Systems

Engineered bio-templated aerogel fibers through gel-spinning and dual-crosslinking strategies, creating hierarchical CNF-TPU-silica structures with thermal conductivity as low as 0.024 W·m⁻¹·k⁻¹ and 294% increase in tensile strength, demonstrating exceptional durability and shape recovery capabilities.

Innovation: Sustainable high-performance insulation for extreme environments

Thermally/Electrically Conductive Polymer/CNT Nanocomposites

Developed a hybrid approach confining carbon nanotubes within nanofibrillated EPDM rubber networks, reducing electrical percolation threshold from 1.5 to 0.25 wt% while simultaneously enhancing crystallization behavior and mechanical properties for flexible electronics applications.

Industry Application: Next-generation flexible electrostatic discharge materials

Polymer Thermo-oxidative Degradation

Systematically investigated active degradation mechanisms during HDPE sintering, identifying two distinct regimes dominated by branching versus chain scission, revealing how oxygen concentration controls pore evolution from surface relaxation to accelerated morphological changes.

Process Control: Preventing degradation in polymer manufacturing

Pore Structure Evolution during Polymer Sintering

Integrating deep learning, reinforcement learning, and neural implicit representations to accelerate materials design, from topology-targeted particle packing to spatiotemporal pore structure prediction.

Manufacturing Insight: Predictive control for additive manufacturing processes

Microcellular Plastics with Enhanced Cell Uniformity and Finer Cell Size

Utilized in-situ rubber nanofibrillation to dramatically improve foaming ability of polyolefins, achieving decreased cell size and increased cell density through interconnected nanofibrillar networks that stabilize cell nucleation and growth during expansion.

Processing Innovation: Superior foam quality for lightweight applications

Foamed Titanium Carbonitride MXene Films for EMI Shielding

Compared hydrazine versus thermal reduction methods for creating highly porous Ti₃CNTₓ MXene films (~74% porosity), achieving unprecedented EMI shielding effectiveness of 118.4 dB through controlled chemistry and morphology optimization.

Record Performance: World's highest EMI shielding in synthetic materials

Enhanced Oxygen Barrier with Morphological and Interfacial Engineering

Developed specialized crosslinking methodologies including silane/moisture and diisocyanate crosslinking, optimizing nanoscale morphology control (50-150nm) for applications from packaging to EMI shielding.

Sustainable Packaging: Extended shelf life with maintained recyclability

Random Particle Packing Generator with Tunable Topology

Developed a Random Sequential Addition algorithm for generating non-spherical particle packings with controllable connectivity, shape, and surface roughness, enabling systematic study of how particle-particle contacts influence sintering and mechanical properties.

Computational Tool: Bridging simulation and experiment in materials designs

AI-Driven Particle Packing

🌐

Deep Reinforcement Learning Framework:

Multi-agent system that learns to generate particle packings with targeted topological properties including porosity, coordination number, and spatial distribution.

Key Innovations:

  • Meta-learning approach for diverse packing objectives
  • Parallel simulation environment for efficient exploration
  • Transfer learning for rapid adaptation to new targets
  • Direct structure-property relationship quantification

Applications:

This framework enables rational design of particulate materials for catalysis, energy storage, filtration, and composite applications by establishing quantitative relationships between controllable parameters and functional properties.

AI Breakthrough: The challenge was creating a reward function that captures the complex relationship between local particle arrangements and global material properties. Our solution: hierarchical rewards that balance local optimization with global objectives.
